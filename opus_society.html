Our mission

Building the pipeline Clemson doesn't yet have.

No organization at Clemson currently prepares underclassmen for careers in public service and government. The Opus Society exists to close that gap — catching students early, before they've missed competitive windows or assumed those careers weren't available to them.

The fellowship

Two years. Two tracks.
One clear direction.

Each year of the fellowship has a distinct focus — moving from foundational professionalization in Year One to substantive career development in Year Two.

Year One

Foundation Track

Building the professional floor. Fellows develop the skills, vocabulary, and awareness to compete for public service opportunities — regardless of major.

  • Resume and LinkedIn for government and policy roles
  • Federal internship and fellowship pipeline overview
  • Professional networking and communication
  • 1-on-1 mentorship from Executive Board members
  • One substantive deliverable per semester
Year Two

Accelerator Track

Translating preparation into outcomes. Fellows develop a career niche, pursue competitive opportunities, and build a real professional network before junior year.

  • Career track specialization and tailored mentorship
  • Competitive fellowship application support
  • Policy memo and briefing document writing
  • Alumni and professional speaker series
  • Capstone deliverable: policy brief or portfolio
Career tracks

Find your direction.

In Year Two, fellows select a track that shapes their mentorship, deliverables, and professional connections. Three tracks. All consequential.

Law & Justice

Preparation for public interest law, civil rights, federal prosecution, and policy litigation. Emphasis on legal writing, advocacy, and the pipeline to law school and beyond.

Public defense Civil rights law Federal prosecution DOJ ACLU

Government & Policy

Preparation for federal agency work, congressional staffing, think tanks, and nonpartisan accountability roles. Emphasis on policy analysis and memo writing.

Congressional staffing Federal agencies GAO Think tanks

International Affairs

Preparation for diplomacy, national security, and international development. Emphasis on foreign policy analysis, area knowledge, and competitive fellowships.

Foreign Service State Department National security Int'l development
Why Opus Society

Different by design.

Underclassmen first

Most programs at Clemson catch students junior or senior year — after the best windows have passed. Opus Society targets first and second years, when decisions about career direction are still being made.

Outcomes, not membership

Fellows leave with tangible deliverables: a polished policy memo, a fellowship application, a professional network. The question "what did you get out of it?" has a concrete answer.

Structural mentorship

Most organizations offer networking opportunities. Opus Society assigns a mentor, sets a meeting cadence, and ties deliverables to that relationship. Mentorship is built into the structure, not left to chance.

Selective by design

A cohort of 25. An application process. A two-year commitment. Membership in the Opus Society means something — to the students who earn it and to the employers and programs who see it.

"There is no clear path to public service careers at Clemson. We are building it."
