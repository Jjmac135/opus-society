FAQ

Common questions.

Who is eligible to apply?

First-year and sophomore Clemson students with a genuine interest in careers in public service, policy, law, or government. Any major is welcome — we are not limited to political science or pre-law students. A GPA of 3.0 or higher is required, though it is not the primary criterion for selection.

What is the time commitment?

Opus Fellows meet as a cohort twice per week and with their mentor at minimum twice per month. Fellows complete one substantive deliverable per semester. The fellowship is designed to be rigorous but manageable alongside a full course load — we ask for consistent, quality engagement, not your entire schedule.

Does the Opus Society have dues?

No. Membership in the Opus Society carries no dues. The fellowship is committed to ensuring that financial circumstance is not a barrier to participation. Any costs associated with optional programming — such as travel — will be subsidized to the extent funding allows.

Do I need to know which career track I want?

No. Track selection happens at the beginning of Year Two. Year One is specifically designed to help you explore and narrow your direction. Apply with genuine interest in public service — we'll help you figure out the rest.

I'm interested in mentoring or speaking. Who do I contact?

Clemson alumni and professionals in public service, policy, law, or government who are interested in mentoring or speaking with Opus Fellows are warmly welcomed. Reach out directly to Josiah Macchi at jdmacch@clemson.edu.

When do applications open?

Applications for the inaugural Fall 2026 cohort open in late August 2026. If you'd like to be notified when applications go live, fill out the interest form linked above — we'll reach out directly.
